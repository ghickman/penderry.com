Having spent years holidaying in Pembrokeshire, many of them at Penderry, Colin and Tina (and their expanding family of children and animals) finally moved here 28 years ago. Penderry, a traditional Welsh farm house with two cottages attached, lies in a unique patch of countryside - seemingly isolated in the beautiful Lampeter Vale, yet only 10 minutes drive from the bustling little town of Narberth. From the meandering river Cwm in the valley to the panoramic hilltop views, Penderry is surrounded by 50 acres of woodland and meadows. The unspoilt farmland boasts an amazing variety of wildlife, flowers, butterflies and birds, as well as the domestic pets, sheep and chickens on the smallholding.

Penderry is surrounded by fifty acres of woodland, meadows, ponds and a river, and is home to an amazing variety of wildlife, including otters, foxes and badgers. Over fifty species of birds have been sighted at Penderry, including red kites, kingfishers and barn owls.

The mixed deciduous woodland has two sites used by many children for Forest Schools. Children from local primary schools come to play, broaden their knowledge, develop confidence and learn to value the countryside. The Chernobyl Children Lifeline also use the woodland as part of their respite holidays for Belarusian children. The Cwm Wood at Penderry was runner-up in the Woods for Wales Award (2007), and won a special award for educational and community use of the woodland.

We farm Penderry as it was probably farmed 100 years ago, aiming to minimise our impact on the environment. We cut our hay late in the year, which allows many species of wildflowers to flourish, and in turn encourages moths and butterflies, providing a source of food for a variety of birds.

The river Cwm meanders along the picturesque valley. In the late autumn, trout can be seen leaping up the small waterfall to go upstream to spawn.

Things to Do

Penderry is ideally situated as a base to explore Pembrokeshire from, whilst enjoying the tranquility of the countryside. The lively little town of Narberth, with its art galleries, cafés and pubs, is only 10 minutes drive away.

Beaches

Pembrokeshire has more Blue Flag beaches and Seaside Awards than any other county in Britain. Penderry is a short drive from Amroth and Wiseman's Bridge.

Coastal Path

No visit to Pembrokeshire is complete without a walk on the Pembrokeshire Coast Path.

Castles

Pembrokeshire is home to many historically rich castles. Pembroke Castle and Carew Castle and well worth a visit.

Art Projects

Colin and Tina have been involved with many children's art, sculpture, and mural projects.

Penderry is a favoured site for eco land art because of its varied landscapes.

Tina takes regular art classes in the farm's well-equipped studio.

Tina Lewis' Paintings

Tina has been inspired by Penderry and it's huge variety of habitats for many years, and the farm has been the basis for many painting exhibitions. Having run her own gallery, Tina now shows her paintings in local Pembrokeshire galleries.

Directions to Penderry

London to Whitland using the M4 and the A40 takes around 4 hours.

Take the left turn into Whitland from the roundabout on the A40, then another left into the High Street signed 'To Tavernspite' - B4328.

Take the first turning on the right after the railway crossing, signed 'To Lampeter-Velfrey'.

Penderry is about 2.5 miles along this lane on the left. The farm track and gate are opposite a cottage with a red tin roof.
